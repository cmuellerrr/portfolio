More things about me

I'm currently an interaction designer at NASA Ames Research Center where I've most notably designed software for the Arc Jet facility (they do the heat shields) and the CoFR review process for future missions (think, "Go/No-go" meetings). Before that I was a lead developer at DIRECTV working on internal software that, among other things, visualized signal flow and equipment status.

I studied Computer Science at Loyola Marymount University and Human-Computer Interaction at Carnegie Mellon University. Across both degrees I worked on projects involving location-based interaction, computer vision, public installations, and head-mounted displays.

I enjoy making things and working with my hands. I like to rock climb and I try to spend time outdoors in places away from congestion.

I do most of my work in Sketch and HTML/CSS/JS but my résumé has a detailed breakdown of my skills and experience. Most of my projects are available on github.
