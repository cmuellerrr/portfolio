More things about me

I'm currently an interaction designer at NASA Ames Research Center where I've worked on things like a mobile task worksheet for the Arc Jet facility and a system for maintaining the flight readiness process for future exploration missions. Before that I was a lead developer at DIRECTV working on internal software that, among other thigns, visualized signal flow and equipment status.

I studied Computer Science at Loyola Marymount University and Human-Computer Interaction at Carnegie Mellon University. Across both degrees I worked on projects involving location-based interaction, computer vision, public installations, and head-mounted displays.

I like to rock climb and I try to spend time outdoors in places away from congestion.

I do most of my work in Sketch and HTML/CSS/JS but my résumé has a more detailed breakdown of my skills and experience.
