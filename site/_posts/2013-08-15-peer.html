Background

Currently, scientists on the ISS are forced to follow rigid, complex procedures throughout their day. These procedures, and the way they are used, cause a significant reduction in efficiency; procedures are static word documents and are only viewable on wall-mounted laptops throughout the station.

Images courtesy of NASA

Concept

After months of research, we developed a prototype procedure viewer utilizing a head-mounted display to allow for hands-free interaction. The prototype also leverages augmented-reality to provide information in place within a crew's work environment. For more details on PEER, see our project website.
