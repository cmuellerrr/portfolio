Product Re-design

I like peanut butter – and I buy my peanut butter from Costco – and those jars are huge. They are larger than the knife I use to get the peanut butter out of the jar. So, when the contents of the jar goes below 50% you end up getting your hand covered in peanut butter. Also, the jars have sharp contours at the top which collect peanut butter but they are inaccessible to your knife.

So, in order to continue buying unnecessarily large jars of peanut butter, I set out to make the jar better.

Avant-garde Architecture

This project was to design a poster for an architecture conference which would be viewed on a large LCD screen in a public area. The text for the poster is related to a conference on the origins of avant-garde architecture in America which was held in New York.

Inspired by the work of Frank Lloyd Wright and Buckminster Fuller, I took a modern, geometric approach. Also considering that the conference was located in New York and is about architecture, the Empire State Building seemed like a natural centerpiece for the poster.
