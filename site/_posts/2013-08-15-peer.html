PEER is a prototype procedure viewer developed in conjunction with the NASA Ames Research Center in order to maximize crew member efficiency while aboard the International Space Station.

View source on github

Background

Currently, scientists on the ISS are forced to follow rigid, complex procedures throughout their day. These procedures, and the way they are useed, cause a significant reduction in efficiency; procedures are static word documents and are only viewable on wall-mounted laptops throughout the station.

Concept

After months of research, we developed a prototype procedure viewer utilizing a head-mounted display to allow for hands-free interaction. The prototype also leverages augmented-reality to provide information in place within a crew's work environment. For more details on PEER, see our ​project website​.
