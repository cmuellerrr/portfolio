Serengeti is a location-aware, social networking application which uses the concept of teams as the driving force behind its usage. Users can join together to claim real-life territory in the name of their team.

Concept

The concept was to turn the idea of a 'check in' into something more meaningful. So, we decided that combining that with group-based conquest would be the perfect avenue to explore.

The point of the application was pretty simple – based on a user’s current location, provide a list of possible locations that were within a set radius. A user could then choose their current location and check-in as a member of a team. Every check-in to a location would garner points for that team; the team with the most points at a location claimed that place as their own.

Implementation

We wanted to be able to show a team’s controlled territory in an interesting way. I ended up implementing a K-means clustering algorithm to group controlled territory into clusters and then used the Graham scan method for determining the convex hull of each cluster. Those convex hulls were then drawn as overlays on a map using the team’s chosen color.

The application was built as both a web application (including a mobile optimized version) and an iPhone application.
